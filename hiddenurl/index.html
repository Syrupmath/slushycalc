The

Slushy Calculator

by Jeffrey Morgenthaler

This calculator prepares cocktail recipes for frozen drink machines. It takes your existing recipe and simultaneously scales it to your desired batch size, while determining what, if anything, needs to be added in order to achieve the proper texture.

Step 1: Name Your Recipe

Enter the name of the cocktail you're converting.

Step 2: Add Ingredients

Slushy machines work best with juice-forward cocktails – think Margaritas, Daiquiris, and similar drinks where citrus or fruit juice makes up a significant portion of the recipe. Spirit-driven cocktails like Manhattans or Martinis aren't a good fit for the slushy format, and as such, this calculator has a hard limit on cocktails with a pre-dilution ABV of 25% and higher.

Add each ingredient with its name, volume, unit, and ABV. Do not include any water dilution, as it will be calculated for you.

Step 3: Enter Refractometer Reading

Proper slushy texture depends on sugar content, and every cocktail starts with a different amount. Before entering your recipe, mix a small sample as written and take a Brix reading with an optical refractometer – this tells the calculator where your drink currently stands so it can determine exactly what adjustments are needed.

Step 4: Select Syrup Type

You'll also need to indicate whether you prefer to use 1:1 or 2:1 syrup for any sugar adjustments. Choose the syrup you'll be using; if no additional sugar is needed, this selection will be ignored.

Enter the total capacity of your slushy machine, or the total batch size you wish to prepare.
